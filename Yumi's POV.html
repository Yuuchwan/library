Yumi's POV

Yumi is a woman caught between two worlds. On one hand, she is still shaped by the rigid, traditional values of her family, and on the other, she has an undeniable attraction to Aya that she can no longer ignore. Throughout the story, Yumi’s POV reflects her internal turmoil—she longs for Aya but feels trapped by the expectations placed on her by society and her family. Her love for Aya is a slow-burn realization, one she keeps hidden for a long time due to fear, guilt, and confusion.

Looking back, Yumi sees that promise as a moment where she unknowingly planted the seeds of her love for Aya.

Even though Yumi doesn’t fully realize it yet, this moment represents a deeper connection that has always existed between them, one that only time would reveal. Yumi’s reflection on this event as an adult is bittersweet; she knows that life and time have changed them both, but a part of her holds onto that naive hope that one day they could fulfill that promise.

No relationship compares to the bond she had with Aya, and it haunts her quietly. She often wonders what Aya is doing, whether she’s thinking of her, and if Aya remembers their promise. Yumi’s POV during this time reflects her yearning and confusion.

There’s an emptiness that only Aya could fill, and it’s something Yumi never fully lets go of. The chapters here would focus on Yumi’s internal voice, full of longing, self-doubt, and a sense of helplessness.

At the same time, Yumi can’t ignore the pull she still feels for Aya.
